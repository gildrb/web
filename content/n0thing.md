# A special client

The 15.11.2019 was a very special day for me. I was in contact with an artist known as VisionOfVIII who I admired a lot. I offered to help him work on his projects as usually do. VisionOfVIII was much more known than me, having worked for top tier organisations in the industry and having a massive fanbase which also meant that he has access to clients that I can't even dream of having. 

### How it happened

I think about this moment a lot, because VIII talked to me in a way that other people at his size would never. If he didn't have time, he would make it. He was going out of his way to talk to the people he cared about. At a certain amount of popularity people stop caring and only talk to their connections which is something I have always despised. A simple "I have no time" or "I do not wish to talk to you" goes a long way. VisionOfVIII was never the type of guy who would ever treat you this way. Instead, he helped me on my journey and tried pushing me up and helped me keep going. I watched his streams, admired his art and appreciated VIII a lot. He casually has received a private message of the former pro player Jordan "n0thing" Gilbert who was proficient at Counter-Strike:Global Offensive but did not have the skillset to fullfil the commission Jordan had. As you would expect from reading this post, VIII recommended me to him. From previously having had Mihai Lupascu as a customer I would have never thought to get another person I knew, watched and respected this soon. Shortly after Jordan Gilbert has private messaged me and commissioned me to fulfill his request. Jordan was in search of a logo. He was initially looking for something typewriter/80s madmen like but didn't tell me front up so I just worked out some ideas. I landed on this custom n0thing mark.

![Early pixel wordmark](media:n0thing-early-pixel-wordmark)

He liked it, but wanted to explore more directions. In hindsight I wish I had gotten further down the typewriter route because I think Jordan hat a great vision.

![Typewriter wordmark direction](media:n0thing-typewriter-direction)

We both had agreed that the typewriter wordmark felt off-balance which it was, but I still think if I had that same task today I would have found a way to make it work. Nonetheless we continued on the pixel wordmark that he had already liked to which I had continued with variations of that same mark.

### Back to pixels

![Pixel wordmark variations](media:n0thing-pixel-variations)

We ended up settling for the version that you can see here.

![n0thing animated wordmark](media:n0thing-wordmark-animation)

Although it might not be the hardest logo to create mechanically, I think we did a good job on finding something that suits him. We wanted something that felt serious and I think we nailed it. It was very easy cooperating with Jordan and I am happy to have gotten to chance to work on it.
